Care and Support to People Living with HIV/AIDS

Prevention of HIV/AIDS Robin

The major and important objective of Chetana Home Based Care Centre in regard to Care and Support program is to prevent the spreading of HIV among the High Risk and general population.

Awareness Programs : Awareness programs through informative session, door to door interaction, counselling and street play are the various methods used to teach and convince the people of HIV transmission.

...

High Risk Area Development Activities : We too introduced a new way of keeping ourselves connected to the high risk areas in our locality. We conducted community meetings and formed Area Development Committee to plan and organize the activities for the improvement and development of their area. We have two gatherings in a month where by the Area development Committee would identify the issues which need to be dealt with and make solutions. We sensitize, motivate and support them technically in identifying the unhealthy practices or issues and in solving it under their leadership. The various activities we conducted were the supplementary education, no to alcohol and gambling, soak pits, plantation of trees, healthy food habits, gathering for strong family and community bond and promise to keep oneself away from unhealthy practices etc

Counselling

Positive and constructive living of those who are infected with HIV can prevent the spread of HIV infection to a certain limit. Those who are infected with HIV are counseling for positive living. They are able to accept a way of living which do not infect anyone else with HIV due to their ignorance and carelessness. The positive counselling urges them to promote those whom they love and with whom they sexually relate to make the HIV test done and take the treatment as early as possible.

Outreach Visits

Out-reach visit is one of the main methods of supporting those who are infected and affected with HIV. These visits help us to understand the PLHAs and their family situations, it supports us to analyze their family situations and provide needful interventions. The out-reach staff along with the field supervisor analyzes the situations and makes the intervention plan to the program coordinator. Thus the out-reach visits bring the clients familiar to the institution. It creates cordial relationships between the institution and PLHAs.

Health Care & Nutrition

Medical support is given to the clients as per their need in terms of cash, medicine and personal assistance. Personnel assistance is given to the person in times of severe illness, admittance in the hospital etc. Monitory assistance is given to the persons where they need travelling expenses to go to the hospitals, ART centers, Doctorâ€™s consultancy fees, medicines etc. As kind we provide them herbal medicines, therapy, supplementary nutrients, food etc.

Herbal treatment is provided to the clients for the common opportunistic infections. Many of our clients are sure and certain of the medicine provided here and they do not seek other medical assistance outside the Centre. The absence of side effects urge the clients to use these medicine whenever they are in need. We too take more initiative to learn about the natural and traditional medicines which has lot of benefits and use them for the better health of the clients.

Supplementary nutrients, protein powder, ration are provided to the clients as per their need considering the physical and mental conditions of the PLHAs. The new born babies and mothers in maternity need more care and nutrition support. This too we provide them. We always envisage the better health of the clients with less infections and more resistance power.

Education & Income Generation activities

With the objective of HIV prevention we organize education to children of High risk population. During their period of studies they become familiar with the life and issues of those who are infected with HIV and their family members. They learn the casus and preventive methods of HIV. These children are medium to educate their parents and family members about HIV. Thus we make efforts to widen the circle of the people in contact to keep them away from the HIV infection and live a healthy life.

Monthly Meetings

Monthly meetings are conducted every month where the PLHAs or their family members participate in it. During these meetings we encourage discussions on HIV, Anti Retro-viral treatment, its side effects, importance of drug adherence, how to live a healthy positive life, importance of school education to their children, current social and political changes and issues etc. During these meetings the PLHAs introduce to us the issues they face in their locality or issues/schemes related to agriculture, insurance, loans etc. Thus monthly meetings are occasions for us to know the developments, encourage and appreciate discussions and open way for change.

Mutual Support Groups

Mutual Support Groups (MSGs) are formed with the intention of financial help and mutual support to one another at their need and requirement. These groups have helped the PLWHA to develop among the PLWHA the attitude of mutual support, assistance, dependency, savings and we feeling.

Advocacy & Legal Aid

Every PLWHAâ€™s family registered in Chetana becomes a part and parcel of this organization. Each and every problem of theirs becomes ours and we are united with them in their struggle for better living and human dignity. We advocate for them within the family for acceptance and for their rights, in work area for livelihood, in Government to get the benefit of government schemes and policies and we stand with the family in getting their children get settled with marriage.

Income Generation Activities

The family who gets adjusted with the physical illness and health problems along with the identification HIV, they need livelihood assistance for ongoing healthy positive living. Thereby we grant them guidance and support for livelihood projects.

Rehabilitation

Life settlement is an important factor in life, which brings happiness, fulfillment and at the same time tension and struggle to parents. It is a time where the PLHA parents struggle mostly, to settle the children in life before anything worse happens to the parents who are infected with HIV and often become prey to opportunistic infections. They also expose themselves to much tension and stress to settle their children in a good family. Here we also identify our major role of function, to alleviate their stress and join them to make it a joyful event. We spent our time with them in helping them to make right discernments and contribute our little to their maximum.

Celebration

1st of December is organized as World AIDS Day to be aware of the prevalence of HIV/AIDS in our locality and in our family. This day we celebrated with hundred women from the low class and daily labor group, giving them classes on health care, food habits, kitchen garden, cleanliness, importance of education and way of rearing the children etc.

14th November is the day for us to gather all the school going and non-school going children of the labor class population. It was a day to tell them they are the leaders of the nation and to motivate them to hold the nation high by being good citizens. It was a day to motivate them with personalities who thought differently and developed the nation by their creativity.There was an input session of small skits and motivational singing. Games and competitions were organized for the children. The program was concluded with a meal. 180 children participated in the program.

Exposure Visits

We organize exposure for PLHA with an objective of teaching and learning through interactions, discussions, working together etc.

About Us

A humanitarian initiative of the Congregation of Sisters of St. Elizabeth (the CSSE Sisters), Started on 14 th june 2009 in response to the alarming situation of people living with HIV / AIDS (PLHA) in India.
